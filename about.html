About civ.works

Rebuilding trust in digital civic engagement.

The Vision

Civic Works is the vision of Golda Velez and George Polisner. Modern democracy faces profound challenges: polarization, disinformation, and declining trust in institutions. Digital spaces often amplify these issues through proprietary algorithms rather than resolve them.

There is a critical need for public-interest platforms designed to promote civic trust, informed deliberation, and authentic engagement. While providing a secure social networking platform, the Civic Works platform will go beyond the conventional by providing:

  • A civic action distribution system
  • Subscriber-customizable algorithms to select and prioritize your social feed
  • Mechanisms to guard against the spread of propaganda, disinformation, and heavily-biased news
  • Ability to participate more fully in democratic processes

Why civ.works?

The name "civ.works" reflects our core belief: civic engagement should work. Not for advertisers. Not for engagement metrics. For democracy itself.

We're building infrastructure that serves the public interest. An ad-free civic social network where:

  • You are not the product
  • Your data is never shared or sold
  • Algorithms serve civic health, not addiction
  • Writers and journalists are compensated fairly
  • Civic actions — not clickbait — are amplified

Our Approach

civ.works combines short-form content (via Bluesky/AT Protocol integration) with long-form Substack-style publishing, creating a unified space for both real-time civic updates and deep analysis. We're building on open protocols because your data should be portable — if we fail you, you should be able to leave.

We're funded by subscriptions, not surveillance. When you pay for the service, the service works for you.
